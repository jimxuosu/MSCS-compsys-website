Basic Info

    The College of Computing (CoC) was established at Georgia Tech in 1990. Initially, it contained only one school (department): Computer Science. Now the college has four main schools involved in research and teaching:
  • School of Computer Science (CS)
  • School of Interactive Computing (IC)
  • School of Computational Science and Engineering (CSE)
  • School of Cybersecurity and Privacy (CP)
    In 2023, a fifth school was created for strictly instructional purposes that handles basic undergraduate courses:
  • School of Computing Instruction (SCI)
    The latest new “SCHOOL” started in FALL 2022 with its program offerings.
  • School of Cybersecurity and Privacy (SCP)
    It offers an M.S. in Cybersecurity which has in-campus and online versions with their own three tracks, etc. As M.S. students, you are NOT affected by what course is taught by faculty from which school. You have to meet the requirements of your specialization.

Degree Requirements

    For more information, visit here.
  • Number of credit hours: 30, regardless of any transfer credits (limited to 6)
  • Non-CoC courses or courses at 4xxx level with a subject code of CS and CSE : at most 6 credit hours.
  • Special problems (8903): at most 3 credit hours; must be within CoC.
  • No maximum on Special Topics courses (8803): these are courses that do not have a regular number yet. Tend to be specialized courses in the research area of the teacher.
  • No course with a grade below a C will count towards graduation.

Specialization Requirements

    For more information, visit here.
  • The school of Computer Science offers only “Computing Systems” as a specialization
  • The schools of IC and CSE have a number of specializations

Expected Core Competencies

    All students are expected to have some undergraduate coursework in:
  • Operating Systems
  • Design and Analysis of Algorithms
  • Formal Languages and Automata Theory
  • Databases
  • Networking and Communications
  • Computer Architecture
  • Human-Computer Interaction
